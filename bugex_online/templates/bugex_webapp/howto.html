    Using BugEx Online


    Submitting your input data

    To submit your input data to BugEx Online, login to the system. On the main page, please provide the code archive file containing your failing program and the failing test case and the fully qualified name of the method of the failing test case. Currently, the provided archive can be either of type JAR or of type ZIP.

    The archive file needs to contain:

    (a) all dependencies of the project,

    (b) both the source code and the binary files (*.java and *.class files).

    The name for the failing test case should have the following format:

    package.containing.test.case.Class#failingTest

    Also you have to confirm that you own all necessary rights to upload the code, before being able to submit your request. After clicking on "Submit" the archive will be uploaded to BugEx Online. Depending on the archive size, this might take a while.

    Please do not close the browser tab and do not disconnect from the internet while the upload is in progress. You will be notified in the browser and via email after the upload has finished.

    On important events, BugEx Online will send you notifications via email, e.g. on successful upload of the archive file, after BugEx finished or if BugEx could not process your request.

    Accessing the BugEx results

    After BugEx finished processing your input data, you will recieve an email containing the link to the results page - Your result will be available to you anytime from now on.

    On the results page, on the left hand side the system will display a list of facts as produced by BugEx, which highlight different important aspects of the failure. You can click on a single fact in the list of facts. On the right side, the system then will then display the code that is associated with the fact, marking those lines to which the fact refers. Additionally, a textual description of the fact is displayed.

    Some variable, method or class may not be defined in the scope of the given source code. This might occur, if one or more necessary source code file are missing in the uploaded archive, or if the variable, method or class belongs to third party code. In this case the system will not be able to display the source code.

    To improve clarity the list allows you to filter facts according to their type. Then the results get visually filtered by the system to only display the facts associated with the types chosen by you.

    Deleting the BugEx result

    If you want to delete the results, please use the link provided in the email together with the link to the result page.

    This will permanently delete the submitted archive, and the BugEx results from the BugEx Online servers.

    Account Management


    Registering with BugEx Online

    To be able to work with the BugEx Online system, please register yourself as a user. For that click on "Registration" below the Sign In form and enter your email address and the captcha. Please, provide a valid email address as it will be used to send the login credentials and other notifications to you. To register, click on "Register" after you filled in the data.

    Changing your email

    If you wish to change your email address associated to your BugEx Online account, please login to the system and access your "My Account" page afterwards. To change your email address, enter the new email address twice in the appropriate "New email address" fields of the form. After that, click on "Submit". For security reasons, a confirmation email will be sent to your both to your old email address and the new one.

    Changing your password

    If you wish to change your password, please access the "My Account" page as described in the last step and click on "Change Password!". A new password will be sent to your current email address.

    Recover password

    If you forgot your password, you can request a new password. To do so, click on "Forgot password?" below the Sign In form. A new password will be sent to your current email address afterwards.


    Advanced Functionality


    Sharing the BugEx result on social networks

    It is possible to share your results from BugEx Online in Facebook or Google+. For that, please click the corresponding button on the result page and follow the instructions from your social network provider.

    This feature is not available yet.

    Downloading the BugEx result

    It is also possible to download the generated BugEx result as an XML document to your local machine. For that, please click the "Download" button on the result page and follow the instructions from your browser.
